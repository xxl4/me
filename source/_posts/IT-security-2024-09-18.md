## What is passive keyless entry (PKE)?
[Read more](https://www.techtarget.com/whatis/definition/passive-keyless-entry-PKE)

Published: Tue, 17 Sep 2024 13:00:00 GMT

Passive keyless entry (PKE) is a feature found in many modern vehicles that allows you to unlock and start your car without using a traditional key. The PKE system uses a small fob or card that emits a unique radio signal. When you approach your car with the fob in your pocket or purse, the system automatically unlocks the doors and allows you to start the car by pressing a button on the dashboard.

PKE systems are more convenient than using a traditional key because you don't have to fumble with keys or worry about losing them. They also provide added security because the system only works with the specific fob that is paired with your car.

Here are some of the benefits of PKE:

* **Convenience:** PKE systems make it easy to unlock and start your car without having to use a key. This is especially convenient when you're carrying groceries or other items.
* **Security:** PKE systems are more secure than traditional keys because the system only works with the specific fob that is paired with your car. This makes it difficult for thieves to steal your car.
* **Keyless entry:** PKE systems allow you to unlock your car without having to use a key. This is especially convenient when you're in a hurry.
* **Remote start:** PKE systems allow you to start your car remotely. This is especially convenient when you're in a cold climate and want to warm up your car before you get in.

If you're looking for a more convenient and secure way to unlock and start your car, PKE is a great option.

* **Self-Healing Networks:** Automation can monitor network health, detect anomalies, and automatically trigger recovery actions to minimize downtime.
* **Dynamic Policy Enforcement:** Automated tools can enforce security policies based on user attributes, device type, and application requirements.
* **Automated Network Monitoring:** Automated systems can collect and analyze network data, providing real-time insights and alerting on potential issues.
* **Zero-Touch Provisioning:** Automation can streamline device deployment and configuration, reducing the need for manual intervention.

**Implementation Considerations:**

* **Interoperability:** Ensure automation tools are compatible with existing SD-WAN infrastructure.
* **Security:** Implement strong security measures to protect automated systems from unauthorized access.
* **Training:** Provide training for IT staff to effectively manage and troubleshoot automated processes.
* **Monitoring:** Establish monitoring systems to track the performance of automated processes and identify areas for improvement.
* **Vendor Support:** Collaborate with SD-WAN vendors to leverage their expertise in automation and optimization.

**Conclusion:**
Automation is a game-changer in SD-WAN optimization, empowering organizations to achieve higher network performance, reduce operational costs, and enhance the overall user experience. By embracing automation techniques, businesses can unlock the full potential of their SD-WANs and drive digital transformation initiatives forward.

## Kubernetes disaster recovery: Five key questions
[Read more](https://www.computerweekly.com/feature/Kubernetes-disaster-recovery-Five-key-questions)

Published: Thu, 12 Sep 2024 11:57:00 GMT

**1. How can I ensure data durability and availability in the event of a disaster?**

* **Implement persistent storage:** Use durable storage mechanisms like persistent volumes (PVs) and persistent volume claims (PVCs) to store critical data on resilient storage backends.
* **Backup and restore mechanisms:** Establish automated backup and restore processes to create point-in-time backups and restore data if needed. Consider cloud-based backup solutions for added durability.

**2. How do I maintain cluster availability during a disaster?**

* **High availability (HA) configurations:** Configure clusters with multiple master nodes and worker nodes to ensure redundancy. HA ensures that the cluster remains functional even if individual components fail.
* **Geographic distribution:** Consider distributing clusters across multiple regions or availability zones to minimize the impact of regional disasters.
* **Cloud-native disaster recovery tools:** Leverage tools like DRP operator or Velero to automate disaster recovery and cluster failover.

**3. How can I recover my Kubernetes cluster after a disaster?**

* **Recovery plan:** Develop a comprehensive disaster recovery plan that outlines the steps to restore and recover the cluster.
* **Testing and simulation:** Conduct regular disaster recovery exercises to validate the plan and identify areas for improvement.
* **Automation and orchestration:** Use automated scripts or tools to minimize manual intervention and streamline the recovery process.

**4. How do I protect against data loss due to hardware failures?**

* **Redundant storage:** Store data across multiple nodes using mechanisms like RAID or erasure coding to mitigate the risk of data loss due to hardware failures.
* **Regular hardware checks:** Implement proactive monitoring and maintenance routines to identify and address hardware issues before they cause significant disruptions.

**5. How can I prevent unauthorized access to my Kubernetes cluster during a disaster?**

* **Strong authentication and authorization:** Implement strong authentication mechanisms (e.g., multi-factor authentication) and role-based access control (RBAC) to prevent unauthorized access.
* **Network isolation:** Isolate the Kubernetes cluster using network firewalls or security groups to restrict access to authorized endpoints.
* **Security audits and monitoring:** Conduct regular security audits and monitoring to identify potential vulnerabilities and implement corrective measures.
