**HSBC Tests Post-Quantum VPN Tunnel for Digital Ledgers**

**London, UK - March 8, 2023** - HSBC has successfully tested a post-quantum virtual private network (VPN) tunnel for digital ledgers, marking a significant milestone in the bank's efforts to protect customer data against emerging threats.

The test, conducted in collaboration with Cambridge Quantum Computing (CQC) and Toshiba, involved establishing a VPN tunnel between two digital ledger nodes using post-quantum cryptographic algorithms. These algorithms are designed to withstand the threat of quantum computers, which could potentially break current encryption methods.

"The development and testing of this post-quantum VPN tunnel is a testament to our commitment to safeguarding our customers' data," said Joe Agius, HSBC's Head of Technology for Global Banking and Markets. "As quantum computing technology advances, we are taking proactive steps to ensure that our systems remain secure and resilient against potential threats."

The test used CQC's IronBridge post-quantum cryptography library, which implements algorithms that have been selected by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) for standardization. Toshiba provided the hardware and networking infrastructure for the VPN tunnel.

"We are delighted to collaborate with HSBC and Toshiba on this groundbreaking initiative," said Ilyas Khan, CEO of CQC. "The successful testing of our post-quantum cryptography technology demonstrates the viability of practical solutions to address the evolving threats posed by quantum computing."

HSBC plans to integrate the post-quantum VPN tunnel technology into its digital ledger platform, which is used for a range of applications, including trade finance, custody services, and wholesale payments.

"The successful implementation of post-quantum cryptography will provide an additional layer of security for our digital ledgers, giving our customers confidence that their data is protected against the threats of tomorrow," added Agius.

## What is email spam and how to fight it?
[Read more](https://www.techtarget.com/searchsecurity/definition/spam)

Published: Wed, 18 Sep 2024 09:00:00 GMT

**Email spam** refers to unsolicited electronic messages sent in bulk, often for commercial purposes or to spread malware. Spam can be a nuisance and a security risk, as it can contain phishing scams, viruses, and other malicious content.

**How to fight email spam:**

**1. Use a reputable email provider:**
Choose an email provider that offers spam filtering and blocking features. They typically have advanced algorithms to identify and filter out spam messages.

**2. Use a spam filter:**
Install a spam filter on your email client or use a web-based spam filtering service. Spam filters analyze incoming emails for suspicious content and block or quarantine those identified as spam.

**3. Be cautious when giving out your email address:**
Avoid providing your email address on untrustworthy websites or to unknown individuals. Spammers often harvest email addresses from online forms or public databases.

**4. Use a "disposable" email address:**
For less important online accounts, consider using a "disposable" email address that you can easily discard later. This helps reduce the amount of spam you receive in your primary account.

**5. Report spam:**
If you receive a spam email, report it to your email provider and to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at www.ftccomplaintassistant.gov. Reporting spam helps train spam filters and identify malicious senders.

**6. Unsubscribe from mailing lists:**
When you receive emails from legitimate businesses that you are no longer interested in, unsubscribe from their mailing lists. This prevents them from continuing to send you promotional emails that may be classified as spam.

**7. Use a strong password:**
Use a strong and unique password for your email account. A weak password makes it easier for spammers to gain access and send emails from your address.

**8. Be aware of phishing attacks:**
Phishing emails often appear to be genuine but lead to fraudulent websites designed to steal sensitive information. Be cautious of emails that request personal information or financial data.

**9. Keep your software up to date:**
Outdated software, including your email client, may have security vulnerabilities that spammers can exploit. Regularly update your software to patch vulnerabilities.

**10. Educate yourself about spam:**
Stay informed about the latest spam techniques and trends. This will help you better recognize and avoid spam messages.

## What is passive keyless entry (PKE)?
[Read more](https://www.techtarget.com/whatis/definition/passive-keyless-entry-PKE)

Published: Tue, 17 Sep 2024 13:00:00 GMT

Passive keyless entry (PKE) is a technology that allows a vehicle to be unlocked and started without the use of a traditional key. Instead, the vehicle uses a key fob that emits a radio signal. When the key fob is in close proximity to the vehicle, the vehicle's sensors will detect the signal and unlock the doors. The vehicle can then be started by pressing a button on the dashboard.

PKE is a convenient feature that can make it easier to access and start your vehicle. It is also a more secure system than traditional keys, as it is much more difficult to duplicate a key fob than it is to copy a key.

* **Enhanced Accuracy:** Automated scripts eliminate human errors, ensuring consistent and reliable configurations.
* **Improved Scalability:** Automation enables the optimization of large-scale SD-WAN deployments with minimal effort.
* **Real-Time Monitoring:** Automated monitoring tools provide constant visibility into network performance, enabling proactive optimizations.

**Key Automation Areas**

**1. Configuration Management:**

* Automate the deployment, configuration, and updates of SD-WAN devices and policies.
* Use templates and centralized management tools to simplify the process and reduce errors.

**2. Performance Monitoring:**

* Implement automated tools to monitor network performance metrics, such as latency, jitter, and packet loss.
* Generate alerts and triggers to initiate optimizations when performance thresholds are breached.

**3. Policy Optimization:**

* Automate the analysis of traffic patterns and application requirements.
* Use machine learning algorithms to dynamically adjust policies, prioritizing critical applications and optimizing bandwidth allocation.

**4. Security Monitoring:**

* Automate the detection and response to security threats, such as DDoS attacks and malware.
* Use automated tools to update security policies and configurations.

**Tools for Automation**

* **Network Management Systems (NMS):** Centralized platforms that provide automated monitoring, configuration management, and policy enforcement.
* **Configuration Management Tools (CMTs):** Software tools that simplify the deployment, configuration, and updates of network devices.
* **Monitoring and Analytics Tools:** Tools that provide real-time visibility into network performance and generate alerts.
* **Machine Learning Algorithms:** Algorithms used for automated traffic analysis and policy optimization.

**Conclusion**

Automation is a key enabler for optimizing SD-WAN deployments. By eliminating manual tasks, reducing errors, improving scalability, and providing constant monitoring, automation empowers IT teams to manage complex SD-WAN environments effectively. By leveraging the right tools and strategies, organizations can unlock the full potential of SD-WAN and ensure optimal network performance and security.

## Kubernetes disaster recovery: Five key questions

**1. What is the scope of your disaster recovery plan?**

This includes defining the applications, data, and infrastructure that need to be recovered. It also includes identifying the potential disaster scenarios that you need to be prepared for, such as hardware failures, natural disasters, or malicious attacks.

**2. What are your recovery point and recovery time objectives?**

The recovery point objective (RPO) is the maximum amount of data that you can afford to lose in a disaster. The recovery time objective (RTO) is the maximum amount of time that it can take to recover your applications and data.

**3. What is your backup strategy?**

This includes choosing the right backup technology, such as snapshots, replication, or backup and restore. It also includes determining how often you will back up your data and where you will store your backups.

**4. How will you test your disaster recovery plan?**

It is essential to test your disaster recovery plan regularly to ensure that it is working as expected. This can be done through simulations, drills, or actual failovers.

**5. What are your ongoing responsibilities for disaster recovery?**

Disaster recovery is an ongoing process that requires regular maintenance and updates. This includes keeping your backups up to date, testing your plan, and training your staff on disaster recovery procedures.
